Thor: Towards Human-Level Whole-Body Reactions under Intense Contact-Rich Environments

Paper arXiv Video Code Data

Abstract

Humanoid robots hold revolutionary potential in service industries, manufacturing, and post-disaster rescue scenarios. However, in these environments, they often encounter situations requiring high pulling forces, such as towing heavy objects or opening fire doors. Enabling humanoid robots to generate high tensile forces while maintaining balance through whole-body coordination remains a challenging problem.To address this issue, we designed a decoupled reinforcement learning framework dividing the robot into upper body, waist, and lower body modules, with each module sharing the full-body observations. A force-adaptive body tilting reward function is introduced, and force disturbances are applied to the end-effectors during training to enhance the maximum pulling capability. Physical experiments were conducted on the Unitree G1 humanoid robot. The results show that the average maximum pulling force reached xx N (approximately xx% of its body weight) when moving backward and xx N when moving forward, representing improvements of xx% and xx%, respectively, compared to the Falcon controller. Additionally, the robot successfully towed a cart loaded with approximately xx kg of weight and opened a fire door. To the best of our knowledge, this represents the highest pulling force achieved by a humanoid robot of comparable weight.
